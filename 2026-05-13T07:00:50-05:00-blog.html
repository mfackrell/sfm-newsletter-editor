God Still Sees

When Ministry Feels Heavy
God Sends Fresh Fire

A week of exhaustion turned into a week of unmistakable God‑moments for JL and Michelle—reminding them, and us, that heaven moves even when we feel hidden.

Key Scripture

“The Lord is close to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit.”

Psalm 34:18 (NIV)

Testimony

The week began with chaos for JL and Michelle as they moved their fifth‑wheel trailer, unsure where they'd be able to park or how they'd manage the logistics. Ministry had felt heavy, lonely, and stretched thin—especially with the ongoing uncertainty around nonprofit funding. Yet at every turn, God kept whispering His presence through unexpected kindness: free parking for their trailer, neighbors jumping in to help, and even a surprise paid-for Thai meal. Each moment was God’s gentle reminder that He sees, hears, and sustains.

In the middle of this whirlwind, they partnered with Abel Ministries during PCB Bike Week—engaging in raw, relational outreach rather than polished sermons. They walked streets and parks, answering questions, praying with strangers, and simply being present. One spontaneous moment birthed a “bag sandwiches” ministry where men who initially brushed them off with “I’m good” suddenly softened, opened up, and asked for prayer within minutes. God was moving through simple acts of love, not platforms.

Michelle carried a quiet ache, feeling unseen and tired from serving behind the scenes. She cried out to God for a fresh word. That night at worship, a stranger she’d never met called her and JL out prophetically—declaring anointing, fire, and a season of stepping boldly into calling. He spoke that the best was still ahead and that within six months, long‑awaited prayers would be answered. It was the exact word she had begged God for. Heaven had heard.

"God will not let you stay hidden when He is ready to bring you into your next season." — A prophetic word that changed everything

What God Revealed

1. God affirms the unseen

Even when fatigue clouds our view, He sends reminders that we are not forgotten.

2. Ministry is love, not performance

The gospel flowed most powerfully through sandwiches and conversations, not stages.

3. God speaks directly to weary hearts

Prophetic encouragement shows God’s precise care for personal struggles.

4. Breakthrough is already on the way

God promised answers within six months—proof that He is orchestrating provision.

Reflection

If you’ve ever felt overlooked, exhausted, or unsure whether God sees your sacrifice, take heart. The same God who met JL and Michelle in chaos, fear, and financial uncertainty is the same God who stands beside you. He is close to the brokenhearted, attentive to the weary servant, and faithful to breathe fire back into tired lungs. You are not hidden—Heaven knows your name.
