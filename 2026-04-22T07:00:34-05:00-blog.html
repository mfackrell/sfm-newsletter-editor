Testimony

From the very beginning of the conversation, there was a strong sense that God was present—moving, speaking, and preparing hearts. The prayer that opened the testimony invited the Holy Spirit to touch every listener, reminding us that God is not done working in our generation. He still intervenes, still redeems, and still meets people right where they are.

One of the most moving updates came from the streets. A longtime friend of the ministry, Jojo, who had battled addiction for years, was unexpectedly brought to a moment of clarity after a brief time in jail. Instead of spiraling, she sobered up, reached out, and said she wanted change. The team rallied immediately—partnering with Ark, Banyan, Oxford House, and volunteers to secure housing, identification, groceries, and probation support. For the first time in a long time, Jojo said she could feel God holding her, keeping her from falling back into the old patterns. This is what hope looks like: people who keep showing up, and a God who breathes life into tiny steps forward.

Another story unfolded with a full-circle miracle. JL shared about walking away from First Christian Church at seventeen when addiction took hold of his life. Decades later, without him asking and without any campaign, that same church chose Salty Farm Ministries for their Easter love offering. Their goal was $40,000. They exceeded it—raising more than $43,000 with more still coming in. It was a reminder that you cannot outgive God, and that He moves through people in ways we could never orchestrate on our own.

Then came another unexpected redemption. A supporter managed to get a Salty Farm “Church Has Left the Building” shirt into the hands of Brandon Lake—at Seacoast, the very place JL and his wife once felt God calling them out to the streets. That message was lifted up on one of the biggest worship stages in the nation, declaring again that the church is not a building—it is a people sent out to those who will never walk through sanctuary doors.

Threaded through every moment was this truth: God is always working behind the scenes. Seeds planted years ago suddenly broke open. Prayers prayed in silence began to bear fruit. What looked dormant was actually being prepared by God’s hands. And all He asks from us is a faithful yes.

Reflection

If you’re tired, discouraged, or wondering whether your prayers are making any difference, take heart. God is moving even when you can’t see a thing. He is shaping stories, softening hearts, and preparing breakthroughs behind the curtain. Your yes matters. Your presence matters. Your prayers matter. And Jesus has not forgotten you—not for a moment.

Call to Action

Surrender today to Jesus. Ask Him to use you, to heal you, and to awaken your heart again. Pray for those trapped in addiction. Serve someone who feels unseen. Let God write a new chapter through you.
