Mission Testimony

When Fear Turned Into Compassion
A Journey Into God’s Heart for the Streets

Fairfield Christian Church senior girls stepped onto the streets of Panama City Beach expecting danger, distance, and discomfort—but God revealed a very different story.

Key Scripture

“The Lord is close to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit.”

Psalm 34:18 (NIV)

Testimony

The Fairfield Christian Church senior girls arrived in Panama City Beach with Salty Farm Ministries carrying a mix of fear, misconceptions, and honest uncertainty. Many expected the homeless community to be rude, closed-off, or even dangerous. Others worried they wouldn’t know what to say or how to handle face-to-face conversations with people they believed were in their situation “by their own choices.”

But almost immediately, God began rewriting their expectations. Instead of hostility, they found joy. Instead of silence, they encountered laughter, gratitude, and deep, heartfelt stories. Wall‑E shared humor and resilience. Jeremy opened up about his journey with warmth. Cage and Ezekiel—who later revealed his real name was Edward—invited them into the complexity of their struggles. Payton shared the gentle, grounding love of his emotional‑support dog, Pepper, who had become his lifeline.

The girls watched the homeless community care for each other with tenderness—sharing food, protecting one another, and even guiding a newly homeless man nicknamed “San Antonio” toward housing resources. They served meals in the rain, watched gratitude overflow during simple laundry cycles, and witnessed firsthand how a hot shower or warm meal could bring someone back to life for a moment. With every interaction, Jesus dismantled their fears and replaced them with compassion.

What began as a mission trip became a revelation: homelessness isn’t a “type” of person. It’s often the result of trauma, loss, mental‑health battles, or simply unlucky circumstances. And behind every face is a story waiting to be heard.

"The moment we stopped judging and simply listened, we saw the people Jesus loves—not problems, not stereotypes, just souls who mattered." — Salty Farm Ministries Mission Team

What God Revealed

Compassion Begins With Listening

Walls break down when we stop talking and start truly seeing the person in front of us.

Small Acts Carry Eternal Weight

A warm meal, a soft word, or a clean shirt can preach the Gospel louder than fear ever could.

Homelessness Is Not an Identity

Most on the streets are battling trauma, mental‑health challenges, or circumstances beyond their control.

Love Looks Like Showing Up

Even in the rain. Even when it’s uncomfortable. Jesus meets people through our presence.

Reflection

Maybe you’ve struggled with fear, judgment, or assumptions. Maybe you’ve avoided the hurting because you didn’t know what to say, or you worried you wouldn't be enough. But the truth is: God uses ordinary people to bring extraordinary hope. If He can use a nervous group of teenagers to reveal His heart on the streets, He can use you—right where you are.

Call to Action

Pray for open eyes. Pray for softened hearts. And ask Jesus to show you the person He’s calling you to see, serve, or simply sit with this week. Let compassion lead you deeper into His love for the broken, the overlooked, and the forgotten.
