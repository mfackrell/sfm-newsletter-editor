A Story From This Month
A man with serious medical needs and no safety net finally found a place to land—through relentless advocacy, God-ordained connections, and a simple promise: “You aren’t going back out on the streets.”

We do it different.

We go to the streets. We meet them where they are. We don’t expect them to have it all together before we provide services. We do whatever it takes to get them where they need to be. We are kingdom focused.

Our Vice President, Ray Sprague, didn’t give up on this man. He reached out to JL and I yesterday, defeated and unsure what to do. Nowhere in our town would provide services. So, I reached out to a resource out of our area, Stephanie at Freedom House - Transitional Housing, who is well connected and also does whatever it takes. With her help, Ray got him to Waterfront Rescue Mission, which is exactly where he needed to be. They are going to help get him connected to everything he needs to get back on his feet.

Here’s where the story gets even crazier: the intake chaplain is Palmer Davis, who happens to be the very first resource we connected with in PCB when we got here (he used to be with Haven House). He is the one that connected us with many other resources in the beginning.

It just amazes me how God connects people long before we know what that connection will produce. In this case, it got a man with medical needs—no physical ability to work, but no disability, no insurance, and no home—into a place that will help him acquire the assistance he needs to get back on his feet, and not just discharge him to the streets with a “good luck and figure it out.”

When Palmer was doing his intake he told this gentleman: “You just relax, we are going to take care of you. You aren’t going back out on the streets.”

Could you imagine the breath he took in that moment? The way his shoulders lowered just a little in knowing he was safe again.

God is ALWAYS working even when we don’t see it. Today, we are thankful for everyone that had a hand in getting this man off the streets and giving him some hope again!

Volunteer Spotlight

Hey Salty Farm Fam, I want to take a second to give a shout out to our volunteers. Day in and day out they show up, wanting nothing more than to show the love of Jesus Christ our Savior!

Last week I received a message from Rebuild Bay County. They knew they were opening the warming center this week, and for the most part everything is covered except showers. So they wanted to know if we would bring the Shower Trailer.

I told her we serve on Monday nights and would not miss our normal serve night, but if 8:30–9:00 wouldn’t be too late I would try to get some volunteers to come with me.

So Friday night I put out the message of what was being asked of us, the location, and the time of night it would be. Let me tell you—instantly I had 3 volunteers jump on it, saying we are there to help serve in whatever way we can.

Today, help me give a big shout out to the 3 volunteers that came to help serve in what I’m calling the Late Night Shift: 8:30–11 p.m.

Brian Loftus, Amy Aubin, Jennifer Lee—thank you for how you all pour your hearts out and choose to do it with Salty Farm Ministries. That goes for all of you that pour out every single day, week in week out. We thank you and love you all.

Just saying yes to the Lord is amazing. Your life will change if you just say yes to Him. Give Jesus your yes today. Watch what He does when you do.
