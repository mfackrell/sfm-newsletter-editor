In the streets — leaving lies and hurts at the cross
Last night, Mark brought the word in the streets. He felt the Lord lead him to talk about how the Word is sharper than any two-edged sword. He asked our friends to write down what lie, hurt, or hang up they held onto in 2025 on one side of an index card. On the other half, he asked them what truth they wanted to carry into 2026. They ripped their cards and left what was at the foot of the cross and behind them. Here are some of those things they laid down.

Please join us in praying for these lives, these hurts, these lies, these souls that want something different. We pray that God meet them right where they are and surround them with His peace, His guidance into truth, His love, His joy, His grace, and His mercy. Let all the things that we all want to leave behind stay behind...stay at the foot of the cross. Let us all open ourselves up to the refining and the filling that God wants to lead us in. Amen.
A Story From This Month
Nichole’s testimony is a powerful picture of Jesus meeting someone in the lowest place and leading them into freedom—now sending her back out as a warrior in the streets.

This is the testimony of one of our volunteers and leaders, Nichole Rambaran...

"2015. Lost. Broken. A hot mess. 20 years addicted to meth/xanex/weed/needles/WHATEVER I COULD OBTAIN. Complex ptsd, agoraphobia, behavioral personality disorder, anxiety, depression. Serious mental instability. Starving for food, life, love, attention. Raising 2 little girls alone, pregnant with a 3rd. I did know how I would buy Christmas this year. This was the year Jesus began to really speak to me. And I was too addicted/broken/LOST to get to Him.

It would take 2 years from this point before I would begin my journey to full surrender. Even in the darkness. The high. The witchcraft. The sexual Addiction. He was speaking to me, wooing me. When I hit the lowest of low, He was waiting. And He got me.

Today.... I belong to Him. Only Him. Sober for 4 years. Thriving. My children are thriving. I am loved/living/validated by Christ. I am a new creation.

When I look at this broken woman, and I am reminded that I was broken as a little girl. And this woman was her. Today, I am a grown woman, and a daughter of God. Not a broken little girl.

The enemy has lost his grip on me. I am Blood Bought. Redeemed. Restored. Healed. Whole. Forgiven. Loved. Called. Honored.

My journey through that hell was so that I could grab others by the hand and pull them out of it, and help them to the foot of the Cross. Where they can find freedom.

After many years of losing to the devil, I realize: the devil got his butt kicked. And my Jesus don't play about me."

Today, she is a warrior in the streets—rescuing others as she leads them to the cross, ushering the broken to freedom.

NEVER feel like God can't use you! The very story of your life could be the very testimony someone needs to hear to pull them from the pit. Never underestimate what God can do in your life and how He can use you afterwards!

If you are struggling with similar battles that she found freedom from, reach out to her! I promise you will not find a better battle partner!

That is what I LOVE about our Salty Farm Fam! There is ZERO judgement, no condemnation. Simply others who have walked through messes, brokenness, addictions, and much more but have simply found our way to Jesus. We don't have it all together. But we definitely don't walk through it alone. Need a community that becomes family? Join us!

Volunteer Spotlight

Hey Salty Farm Fam, I want to take a second to give a shout out to our volunteers—day in, day out they show up, wanting nothing more than to show the love of Jesus Christ our Savior!!

So last week I received a message from Rebuild Bay County. They knew they were opening the warming center this week, and for the most part everything is covered except showers!! So I had met her the week prior and told her what we do as a ministry. So she wanted to know if we would bring the shower trailer.

I told her we serve on Monday nights and would not miss our normal serve night, but if 8:30-9:00 wouldn’t be too late, I would try to get some volunteers to come with me. So Friday night I put out the message of what was being asked of us, the location, and the time of night it would be. Let me tell you, instantly I had 3 volunteers jump on it, saying we are there to help serve in whatever way we can.

So today help me give a big shout out to the 3 volunteers that came to help serve, in what I’m calling the Late Night Shift!!! 8:30-11 p.m.

Brian Loftus, Amy Aubin, Jennifer Lee—thank you for how you all pour your hearts out and choose to do it with Salty Farm Ministries. That goes for all of you that pour out every single day, week in week out. We thank you and love you all.

Just saying yes to the Lord is amazing. Your life will change if you just say yes to Him. Give Jesus your yes today. Watch what He does when you do.

How You Can Pray

    If you could please lift him up in your prayers with us, we would greatly appreciate it! God knows his every need and we are standing on the truth that God has a big plan for his life. Thanks, SFM Fam, for standing in the gap with us!
