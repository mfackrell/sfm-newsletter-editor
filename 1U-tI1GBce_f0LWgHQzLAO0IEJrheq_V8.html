Story of the beginning...

In October 2021, God put a huge calling on our hearts to sacrifice the material and excess to serve those with little to none. He was calling us into a three part mission: mobile food ministry, mobile shower ministry, and a tiny home community. We had no idea how we were going to do it, but we gave Him our YES and stepped out in faith in a big way! We spent another year in Illinois, selling everything we owned (including our dream home) and bought a motorhome to live in. We relocated with two of our kids to Panama City Beach, FL from Southern Illinois to serve the veterans and brothers & sisters living on the streets...what we refer to as "our friends on the street". Believe us when we say that we prayed hard for God to lead us to the right place. I mean, the beach is amazing, but we didn't want it to be where we wanted to go, we wanted it to be where God wanted us to go! He kept confirming this is where He needed us.

Upon arriving in Panama City Beach, we quickly learned why. The number of displaced people in this area just keeps rising with the aftermath of Hurricane Michael and the rising housing costs. Furthermore, the homeless shelter got wiped out by the hurricane and, frankly, there aren't many resources for them in this area.

Salty Farm Ministries hit the streets with 100 sandwiches on Christmas Day of 2022. We didn't have a clue what we were doing. We just headed out looking for people in need. We met our first friend, Doc, on the side of the road heading to buy a pack of cigarettes. We sat on the side of the road with him, listened to his story, bought him a pack of cigarettes, and he shared where more friends could be found. That day we served 12 people. Twelve. If we are honest, we were a little disappointed! "God, we sold everything for twelve?" But God in all His goodness, gave us exactly what we could manage at that time. Those twelve became the foundation of relationships and trust in the streets with many others.

God has been in it from the very beginning and will always lead our way. We still say "we have no idea what we are doing" and we like it that way...because that means we rely fully on God!

A Story From This Month
We mourn the loss of our friend Calvin, while also holding tightly to the hope that he knew Jesus and is now home.

Just got word that Calvin passed away. From what we have gathered, he was invited to go out on the boat with the owners of his work and had a heart attack on the dock and fell into the water before meeting up with everyone. He was pulled out of the water from some friends of ours and taken to the hospital. John Johnson was on his way to pray over him and blow his shofar. When John would randomly blow his shofar on the beach, Calvin would miraculously show up. However, John didn't make it in time to pray for him.

But the good news is Calvin knew Jesus so he is no longer homeless but dancing on the streets of gold in heaven in his eternal home.

Its moments like this that make what we do difficult. Knowing he knew the Lord lightens the heaviness in our hearts. We will certainly miss Calvin and his servants heart, his smile, his hugs, and his sweet spirit.

He was one of the friends that set up our Thursday serve nights and worked with JL on several odd jobs helping friends of ours. He was a hard worker. But beyond that he was also so kind and such a giver to so many.

This loss will certainly leave a mark on many of our hearts. 💔 We mourn the loss of our friend but celebrate his victory in eternity. Please keep our friends, Calvin's family, and our team in your prayers.
