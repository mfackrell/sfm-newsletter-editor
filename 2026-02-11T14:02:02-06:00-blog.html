A Story of Redemption

From Broken Roots to
A Life Rebuilt in Christ

Max’s journey shows how Jesus meets us in the darkest places and rebuilds what life tried to destroy.

Key Scripture

“I press on toward the goal to win the prize for which God has called me heavenward in Christ Jesus.”

Philippians 3:14 (NIV)

Testimony

Max grew up without church, moving constantly within a military household that never allowed his identity to settle. With no spiritual foundation, he built his own version of morality—one that shifted with circumstances and emotions. When his parents divorced at sixteen, the fracture planted deep lies in his heart: love wasn’t real, life held no meaning, and he alone had to govern his path.

Those lies took root quickly. School didn’t matter, so he stopped going. His grades crashed. Weed and alcohol became daily habits that soon spiraled into pills, cocaine, meth, and opioids. Addiction swallowed him whole, dragging him into near‑death moments and a world of crime, despair, and numbness. Yet God, even then, was planting seeds. The birth of his son, the deaths of his sister and friends, and glimpses of his own destruction began shaking him awake.

One night, watching films about Jesus, everything clicked—this story wasn’t myth. Jesus was real. The cross was personal. The gospel was for him. On May 4, broken and desperate, he stepped into Evans Training Center—a Christ‑centered regeneration program. On the bus ride there he prayed the rawest version of the Romans Road he knew. Withdrawal, trauma, pride, and spiritual battle met him immediately, but so did the healing power of Scripture, confession, and brotherhood that spoke truth in love.

Today Max stands sober, restored, and serving as a worship leader with Sojourner Collective. He is an engaged father, a minister of hope, and a man learning daily that salvation isn’t perfection—it's dependence. His life now bears mercy, peace, and joy, even in the imperfections. He carries a message born from fire: pressing on in Christ is the only way through, and “even if it doesn’t work out, God works it out of you.”

"Even if it doesn’t work out, God works it out of you." — Max

What God Revealed

God pursues us even in deep rebellion

Max’s darkest moments were interrupted by God’s mercy and conviction.

Truth uproots lifelong lies

The gospel replaced the lies of worthlessness and self-governance.

Community strengthens transformation

Brotherhood, confession, and discipleship became tools of healing.

Sanctification is a journey

Following Jesus isn’t instant perfection—it’s continual pressing on.

Reflection

If you feel trapped, ashamed, or convinced that your story is beyond repair, hear this: Jesus has a way of stepping into the places you thought were too ruined for redemption. Max’s story is proof that the gospel still rescues, restores, and rebuilds lives brick by brick.

Call to Action

Invite Jesus into the broken places. Pray honestly. Reach for community. Don’t walk alone—healing grows where truth and love meet.
