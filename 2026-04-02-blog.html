Redeemed for More
Max’s Journey from Addiction to Worship

From the grip of addiction to the grace of worship, Max’s story is a living reminder that Jesus still saves, still redeems, and still restores what was lost.

Key Scripture

“Not that I have already obtained all this, or have already arrived at my goal, but I press on to take hold of that for which Christ Jesus took hold of me.”

Philippians 3:12 (NIV)

Testimony

When Max sat down with Salty Farm Ministries, the studio felt like family. Even the ministry dog, Gizmo, seemed to sense the weight of grace in the room. Max, a longtime part of the ministry, came to share how God pulled him from the depths of addiction and called him to launch a new worship ministry — Sojourner Collective — devoted to house worship nights and authentic encounters with the Holy Spirit.

Max didn’t grow up in church. Aside from a brief brush with the LDS faith, he spent much of his youth searching for identity in an unstable military household. Constant moves left him guarded and adaptable, a chameleon trying to fit wherever he landed. By the time his family settled in Panama City, freedom and poor influences pulled him from a high-achieving student to a life of truancy, suspended licenses, and escalating addiction. Opiates, cocaine, meth—each step further numbed the void he couldn’t name.

At sixteen, his parents’ divorce shattered what little stability remained. Lies began to take root: that love wasn’t real, that nothing lasted, that he alone could steer his life. Those lies paved a road through loss, crime, and near-death experiences. Even as he heard a quiet inner voice whisper, “What are you doing? I made you for more,” he buried it beneath the noise of addiction.

One Easter morning, dopesick and desperate, Max turned on a movie about Jesus. As he watched the crucifixion, something broke. The cross wasn’t just a story — it was personal. The resurrection was real, and the Savior he’d ignored was reaching out to him. That moment of revelation set him on a path that would change everything.

On May 4th, Max entered Evans Training Center, a faith-based regeneration program for men. He refused to call it rehab — “the program is Jesus, and the curriculum is the Bible.” On the bus ride there, he opened a pocket Bible and stumbled through the Romans Road. His prayer wasn’t polished, but it was real. That raw surrender invited God into the darkest corners of his life. The early days were intense — spiritual warfare, offense, buried trauma — yet through confession, brotherhood, and truth spoken in love, healing began to flow.

Today, Max’s story is one of ongoing sanctification, not instant perfection. God has restored his relationships, especially with his children. He now leads in worship, serves in ministry, and walks in the joy and peace that only mercy can give. The same man who once chased every counterfeit now rests in the presence of the real thing — Jesus Christ. Max presses on, reminding others that even when life doesn’t work out as planned, “God is working it out of you.”

"Even when it doesn’t work out, God is working it out of you — shaping you for the life He’s called you to live." — Max, Sojourner Collective
