Love in Action

Everyone Can Do Something
For Someone Else

A group of graduating seniors learns that ministry is less about giving things and more about giving yourself—just like Jesus did.

Key Scripture

“Whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me.”

Matthew 25:40 (NIV)

Testimony

JL sat down with a group of high school graduates from Central Christian Church who spent a week serving on the streets of Panama City. What began as a simple mission trip quickly unraveled every stereotype they had carried about homelessness, ministry, and what it means to love like Jesus.

They arrived expecting to hand out food, water, and hygiene products. Instead, they found men and women who were hungry not for handouts, but for conversation, dignity, and connection. The teens learned that people experiencing homelessness often have profound stories—stories rooted in family loss, unexpected tragedy, and circumstances that could happen to anyone.

As they listened, prayed, and returned day after day, relationships formed. What surprised them most was how deeply their new friends simply wanted to be seen. They also witnessed how some churches turned people away because they were “dirty” or “looked rough,” and this broke their hearts. It reshaped their understanding: the church is not a building—it’s people living out the love of Christ.

"When we showed up, they didn’t ask for stuff—they asked for us." — Central Christian Church Students

What God Revealed

People Need Presence More Than Products

Real ministry begins when we slow down and truly see people.

Relationship Builds Trust

Returning without an agenda opened doors to prayer and healing.

Anyone Can Lose Everything

Homelessness was rarely the result of laziness—it was often loss.

The Church Must Be Jesus’ Hands

God calls His people to step outside the building and into the streets.

Reflection

Maybe you’ve felt too broken, too tired, or too unworthy to help anyone. But the truth is this: God can use you exactly where you are. Ministry is not reserved for pastors or missionaries. It happens whenever you choose to see someone the way Jesus sees them. If these young men can step into hard places with courage, so can you. Someone near you is waiting for compassion, hope, and a simple conversation that reminds them they matter.

Call to Action

Pray for eyes that see people the way Jesus does. Ask Him to soften your heart toward the unseen and overlooked. Then take one step—serve, encourage, give, or go. Every act of love plants seeds of hope. And if you feel led, partner with Salty Farm Ministries to support local outreach.
