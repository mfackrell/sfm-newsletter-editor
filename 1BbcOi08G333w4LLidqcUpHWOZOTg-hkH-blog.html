When Obedience Opens the Door
Saying Yes to God in the Cold

Key Scripture

“Whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me.”

Matthew 25:40 (NIV)

When a rare Florida cold snap threatened the safety of vulnerable neighbors, the county and Rebuild Bay opened the doors of the A.D. Harris Learning Center as an emergency warming shelter. With little notice and no time to plan, Salty Farm Ministries felt the Lord tugging on their hearts—and they simply said yes. That yes became a lifeline for dozens of people seeking warmth, dignity, and hope.

Testimony

The cold front hit harder than expected, pushing shelters and local agencies to open emergency spaces for those living outside. At the last minute, Salty Farm Ministries received the call: could they serve? There was no time to prepare, no weeks of planning—only the opportunity to show the love of Jesus in real time. And with full hearts and open hands, they said yes.

After an already full outreach Monday morning, they stayed from 8:30 to 11 p.m., providing around 20 warm showers and nearly 30 visits to the clothing closet. Those simple acts—soap, towels, clean clothes—became moments of dignity, relationship, and prayer. Then on Friday, they returned again, serving a record 29 showers with the help of faithful volunteers, including teens eager to be the hands and feet of Christ.

Through exhaustion, long hours, and the unexpected, God revealed once again that ministry is not about transactions—it’s about people. It’s about discipleship, relationship, and showing up when God calls, even if it costs something. Because to Him, every single person matters.

What God Revealed

  • A simple yes can change someone’s life—and often ours too.
  • Ministry is people-focused, not task-focused.
  • God multiplies what we offer, even when it feels small.
  • Every act of love echoes the heart of Jesus to the hurting.
  • One person matters deeply to God—and should to us.

“Give God your yes, and He will place you exactly where someone needs His love.”

Reflection

Maybe you’ve felt God nudging you to step into something uncomfortable—something that requires faith more than preparation. The truth is, God does His greatest work when we are simply willing. He uses ordinary people to bring extraordinary hope. If you’re walking through brokenness, or feeling too weak or unqualified, remember: God isn’t looking for perfection. He’s looking for availability. And He loves you more deeply than you may realize.

Call to Action

Pray and ask God where He is calling you to say yes. Step into community, seek healing, and let Jesus guide your next step. If you feel led to partner with Salty Farm Ministries—through serving, giving, or providing resources—visit saltyfarministries.org. Together, we can reach one more person with the love of Christ.

Closing Prayer

“Lord, give us the courage to say yes when You call. Open our eyes to see those who need Your love and use our hands to serve them. Let our obedience bring hope, healing, and Your presence to the hurting. Amen.”
