When a Church Says Yes
The Ripple of Local Mission

Key Scripture

“The harvest is plentiful but the workers are few.”

Matthew 9:37 (NIV)

At Shiloh Baptist Church in Chipley, Florida, God used the Global Impact Celebration to spark something powerful—an awakening to local mission. Through prayer, outreach, and a willingness to say yes, the church saw hearts open, misconceptions shatter, and new laborers rise up to serve the broken, hungry, addicted, and hurting right in their own community.

Testimony

Before committing to partner with Salty Farm Ministries, the pastor of Shiloh Baptist stepped out in faith and personally joined in street outreach, wanting to see the ministry firsthand. God confirmed the partnership quickly, and from that moment, the church’s missions committee—Michelle, Misty, and Jennifer—began seeking the Lord about how to stir their congregation toward obedience.

They realized many believers felt they “couldn’t go overseas,” so missions felt out of reach. Instead of accepting that limitation, the committee shifted the focus: what if missions didn’t require a passport? What if the mission field was the streets, neighborhoods, and shelters within minutes of their own homes? That single shift changed everything.

During the Global Impact Celebration, Shiloh created space for real relationships. Local partners—including Salty Farm, Habitat for Humanity, Freedom Collective, a disaster relief team, and a veterinary mission—were invited to share their hearts. The response was immediate: people packed crackers for homeless neighbors, signed up for orientations, and took their first steps toward serving. God opened doors for new partnerships, like human-trafficking awareness posters through Freedom Collective and a veterinary team exploring street-pet care for the unsheltered. Even deeper, old assumptions crumbled as attendees realized homelessness isn’t a choice—it's often the tragic result of trauma, addiction, or generational pain. Shiloh was reminded that their responsibility wasn’t to fix people but to love them like Jesus and plant seeds only He can grow.

What God Revealed

  • Local missions matter just as much as global missions.
  • You don’t need a passport to be obedient to the Great Commission.
  • People struggling with homelessness and addiction need compassion, not assumptions.
  • Love plants seeds—God alone brings the growth.
  • When a church says yes, God multiplies the laborers.

“You’re not called to fix people—you’re called to love them like Jesus.”

Reflection

Maybe you’ve felt unqualified or unsure where to start. Maybe brokenness feels too heavy or too messy. But God isn’t asking you to fix what only He can heal. He’s asking you to show up with compassion, humility, and willingness. Like Shiloh, you can make your “yes” available to Him. And when you do, He will lead you to the people who need His love most—right where you live.

Call to Action

Pray for God to open your eyes to the needs around you. Repent of any assumptions you’ve held about people living in addiction or homelessness. Ask Jesus to fill you with His compassion and courage, and then take one simple step toward serving your community. You don’t need to travel far—your mission field may be just one street away.

Closing Prayer

“Jesus, give me Your eyes for the broken, Your heart for the lost, and Your courage to say yes. Lead me to the people You want me to love, and let my life be a seed that brings glory to You. Amen.”
