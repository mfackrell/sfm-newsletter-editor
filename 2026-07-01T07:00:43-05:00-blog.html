Mission Reflections

Learning to See the Unseen
When We Say Yes to God

A mission-team encounter that revealed God’s heart through prayer, presence, and unexpected friendship.

Key Scripture

“Truly I tell you, whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me.”

Matthew 25:40 (NIV)

Testimony

When the Central Christian Church team from Mount Vernon, Illinois stepped onto the streets of Panama City Beach with Salty Farm Ministries, they expected to serve others. What they didn’t expect was to be served in return by the very people they came to help.

The team prayed together, walked the streets, and met what they began calling “friends on the street”—men and women who had stories of heartbreak, loss, addiction, and unexpected circumstances that pushed them into homelessness. The team realized quickly that these individuals weren’t defined by their struggles. They were people—image-bearers—often pushed into hardship by a single devastating moment.

Instead of approaching the trip with a goal to fix anyone, the team embraced relationship. They listened. They prayed. They sat with people who felt unseen. And in that sacred space, God revealed the beauty of simple presence. Time and time again, those they met offered encouragement, laughter, and kindness. Blessing flowed in both directions.

What they witnessed from Salty Farm Ministries left a deep impression: a 24/7 posture of obedience. A lifestyle of saying “yes” to the Holy Spirit—no matter the hour, no matter the inconvenience. That posture challenged the team to carry the mission home with them. They learned that the mission field isn’t just PCB. It’s every street, workplace, and neighborhood. It’s every moment we choose to stop, look someone in the eyes, and see them the way Jesus does.

"When we slowed down long enough to see people, we realized God was already there—waiting for us to show up and say yes." — Central Christian Team Reflection

What God Revealed

People Need Presence, Not Quick Fixes

Healing begins when someone is willing to listen and love without an agenda.

Circumstances Can Break a Life—But Christ Restores

Many experiencing homelessness or addiction carry deep wounds rather than failures.

Obedience Opens Doors Only God Can Use

A simple yes can shift someone’s eternity.

The Mission Field Is Everywhere

Every city, every workplace, every street corner holds someone who needs the love of Jesus.

Reflection

Maybe you feel unseen. Maybe you feel like your story has been shaped by circumstances outside your control. Or maybe you’re longing for a deeper purpose—something more than the routine of everyday life. This testimony reminds us that Jesus meets us on the streets, in the broken places, in the slow moments where love has room to breathe. He invites you to step into a life of courage, compassion, and faith-filled obedience. You don’t have to change the world—just be present where He places you.

Call to Action

Say yes to God today. Pray for someone. Slow down enough to see the unseen. Let the Holy Spirit lead you into deeper compassion. And if God stirs your heart, partner with Salty Farm Ministries—through prayer, support, or joining a future mission trip.
