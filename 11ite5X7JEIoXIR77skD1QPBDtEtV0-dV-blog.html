Testimony

When JL and Relle sat down with Hayden from Central Christian Church in Mt. Vernon, Illinois, their conversation overflowed with the joy and awe of God’s work during Central’s twelfth mission trip to the streets of Panama City Beach. What began as a simple desire to serve has become a yearly pilgrimage—one that strengthens their spiritual family, ignites faith in first-time missionaries, and reminds the church what it means to love like Jesus.

This year, Central launched one of its largest supply drives yet. From new socks and underwear to hygiene items, clothes, and essentials for the shower trailer and giving closet, the church and its surrounding community rallied beyond expectation. High school students, teachers, local families, and every campus of Central joined together. Bags piled high. Classrooms became donation hubs. Generosity overflowed.

And on the streets of Panama City Beach, those donations transformed lives. A shower. A clean pair of socks. A fresh haircut. Simple acts—yet profoundly sacred. Team members watched men and women stand a little taller. Smile a little easier. Approach conversations they once avoided. The stigma surrounding the word “homeless” melted when people were seen, loved, and treated with dignity.

"We are washing souls one shower at a time, not because of who we are, but because Jesus first washed ours." — Central Mission Team

Reflection

Maybe today you feel unseen, overlooked, or unworthy. Maybe you're carrying addiction, shame, or brokenness that keeps you from looking others in the eye. Hear this: Jesus sees you. He hasn’t forgotten you. And just as He washes the feet of the weary, He longs to wash your soul clean and restore your dignity. You matter deeply to Him—and you always will.
