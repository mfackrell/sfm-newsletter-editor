Testimony

From Broken Identity
to Redeemed Purpose

Max’s journey from addiction and despair to a life anchored in Christ’s mercy.

Key Scripture

“He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion until the day of Christ Jesus.”

Philippians 1:6 (NIV)

Testimony

Max grew up without church, without spiritual grounding, and without a stable identity. His military-family childhood meant constant transitions—new towns, new faces, new rules. Nothing stayed long enough to shape who he was becoming. So he shaped himself, building a fragile identity on shifting morals and a belief that he alone must decide what was right.

When his family moved to Panama City, everything tilted. Surrounded by the wrong crowd and reeling from his parents’ divorce, Max watched his father weep into a beer can. In that moment, a set of lies carved deep into his heart: love isn’t real, nothing lasts, the future is pointless, and survival means governing yourself. Those lies quickly took root, pulling him into truancy, failing grades, and eventually a fast-burning addiction to multiple drugs. Crime, danger, and death tightened around him like a vice.

Yet even at his lowest, a whisper inside told him he was made for more. One Easter, sick and withdrawing, Max watched films about Jesus. Something shifted. For the first time, he understood the gospel as history—real, grounded, true. Jesus wasn’t a myth; He was God. And the cross was personal. It was for him. On May 4, Max boarded a bus to a faith-based regeneration program. There, detox, trauma, and pride battled for control. But truth-in-love community and the steady voice of Scripture—especially the Romans Road—began to heal what addiction had shattered.

Today, Max walks in sobriety, joy, and responsibility. He is an involved father and a worship leader with Sojourner Collective, helping host worship nights and serving in ministry. He still faces struggles, but now he faces them with peace, mercy, and the steady presence of Christ. His message is clear: Christians are not perfected instantly. We press on. God takes what was meant for evil and turns it for good—and even when life doesn’t work out, God works it out of you.

"Even when your life doesn’t work out, God is faithful to work it out of you." — Max

What God Revealed

Identity without Christ collapses

Max built his own identity, but only Jesus provided a foundation strong enough to hold him.

God pursues us even in addiction

In the middle of dopesickness and despair, God met Max with truth.

Healing happens in community

Truth spoken in love and Scripture-based discipleship rebuilt his soul.

God finishes what He starts

Max’s life is proof of Philippians 1:6—God continues the work long after salvation.

Reflection

Maybe you’re reading this with old wounds, heavy shame, or a heart that feels too far gone. But the same Jesus who met Max in his brokenness is the Jesus who stands ready to meet you now. No matter how deep the addiction, how dark the past, or how numb your heart feels, Christ is not intimidated by your story. He loves you. He sees you. And He is willing to rebuild you from the inside out.

Call to Action

Take a step toward Jesus today. Pray. Reach out. Join a community that tells you the truth in love. You are not beyond hope, and you are not meant to walk alone. Christ has more for you.
